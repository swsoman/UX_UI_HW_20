A Website Redesign for a Non Profit Organization

Role: UX UI Designer
Type: Educational, Group Project
Responsible for User Research, Definition, Ideation, Redesign and Rebranding the style guide ,Prototyping of the wireframes
Project Timeline : 3 weeks

Project Summary
Peel Animal Rescue Center a.k.a PARS was choosen because they work towards this incredible cause of finding new homes and families for rescue animals.
Their website needed a whole new UX design approach especially making the adoption process a 100% online. This was mostly something that was necessary given the fact that this organization works 100% online during Covid-19 Pandemic.
One of the main project goal was to design an experience for adoption of a rescue animal in the new normal world senario.

User Research
User research was conducted with user 6 interviews and 7 online survey responses.

User interview objective:
To understand the functional obstacles users have to overcome and measure opportunities for improvement
Online Survey objective:
To understand the usability of current website and quantify the number of users able to navigate the website successfully
User Persona
Feedback from user research was used to put together a User Persona for the PARS Website

User Insight

"Catherine needs to find a companion for her children and their marriage. She wants to lighten the dynamic of her home due to Covid-19. This is mainly because of a combination of fear and safety concerns when it comes to her kids."
"Catherine is an active donor to various non-profit organizations. She wants to continue this tradition by supporting Peel Animal Rescue and feels adopting a pet will be a great idea to keep her children busy and happy. "

Problem Statement

"We believe that by redesigning the website we can make the process of adopting a pet easier to comprehend and complete for Catherine. Catherine was frustrated with her initial experience in adopting a pet. She was unclear with how the adoption process works. We want to redesign the Peel Animal Rescue website to help Catherine confidently proceed in the adoption process. "

Stroryboard
Storyboarding was used to empathize with Catherine, our User Persona. How could Catherine Adopt a pet during Covid-19 Pandemic?

Competitor Analysis
Three similar Non profit organization websites were studied to compare the adoption process and animal profile features.
A SWOT Analysis was done to narrow down the opportunities for PARS.

Feature Prioritization
MOSCOW method was used to do a feature prioritiztion.

Usability Testing Plan

Test on 5 users to determine if website is organized the way easily for users to navigate and find their information.

Usability Test Tasks


  • Header and Footer navigation

  • To find information about how to adopt a pet and navigate to FAQ page

  • To adopt a dog by using online application form and get contact information

Usability Testing Feedback

Test on 5 users to determine if website is organized the way easily for users to navigate and find their information.

  • Navigation is working, but color contrast need to be improved, and also login button need be redesigned.
  • Too much words on How to Adopt page and there is no hierarchy showing the User about adoption steps and embedded links.
  • The layout in Dog’s profile page need to be improved, should use color contrast and hierarchy to show the highlighted information and what is the intention of page.

Key Takeaways
These were some of the takeaways from this project
  • Visual hierarchy and contrast are important to the design in order to effectively communicate contents to Users
  • Only presenting essential information to allow users find what they need
  • All of the design choices must reflect what the client stands for.
  • Implementing responsive web design to allow digital content be accessible in different screen sizes
